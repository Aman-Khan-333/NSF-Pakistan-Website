Meet the Founder

Professor Dr. Shakil Jehangir Malik

Professor Dr. Shakil Jehangir Malik is the founder of the Neuro Sciences Foundation Pakistan and a renowned neuro-psychiatrist with more than four decades of global clinical experience. Across a career spanning continents, he has devoted himself to one conviction — that mental illness is treatable, and that no one should be denied care because of where they were born or what they can afford.

Long before mental health entered Pakistan's national conversation, he was challenging the stigma, fear, and neglect that surrounded it — championing humane, community-centred treatment built around dignity, family involvement, and care delivered where people actually live.

From a single clinic, that vision has grown into the country's largest free mental health network — reaching more than 25 locations, from urban centres to the remotest villages of Balochistan.

“Where you were born should never determine whether you receive care”

He never accepted that serious mental illness was untreatable, or that the poor should simply go without. Instead he brought expert psychiatric care into people's own homes and communities — reaching patients even the country's tertiary hospitals could not.

Inspiration & Vision

A Legacy of Excellence in Neuroscience

The Neuro Sciences Foundation was built on a vision to advance neurological care, research, and education. Inspired by Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan and led by Prof. Shakil Jehangir Malik, it reflects a lifelong commitment to innovation, compassion, and lasting impact.

Around that vision he gathered a team of highly skilled professionals — consultant psychiatrists, clinical psychologists, therapists, and social workers — united in delivering state-of-the-art neuropsychiatric care to the socially deprived and marginalised.

“Innovation, compassion, and lasting impact — the foundation of every life we touch”
